

  			  						Position Title: Data Scientist     						Position Schedule: Full Time    						Location:  Vienna, VA    						Minimum Clearance: Top Secret    						     						Description of Duties:    						Strategic Operational Solutions, Inc. (STOPSO) is seeking candidates for Data Scientist position.  At STOPSO, we deliver innovative systems, solutions and services in support of the critical missions of the Intelligence Community, Department of Defense, Department of Justice, and Department of Homeland Security along with other federal agencies seeking to ensure the safety and security of our nation.    						     						Support Requirements:    						Develops and applies methods to identify, collect, process, and analyze large volumes of data, with the goals of building and enhancing TSC business intelligence products, models, processes, and systems  							Conducts data mining and retrieval and apply statistical and mathematical analyses to identify trends, solve analytical problems, optimize performance, and gather intelligence  							Qualified candidates must be able to visualize information using a range of tools (e.g., STATA, SAS, R), develop scripts and algorithms, create explanatory and predictive models, and conduct comparative analyses to address complex business, operational, and intelligence problems  							Works with the TSC to clarify customer needs throughout these activities to ensure that their work supports effective management decision-making and furthers the TSC mission.    							The government supervisor may determine other Data Scientist duties to respond to the FBI/TSC mission  							Establishes priorities and plans analytical projects and/or research, aligning project goals to partner or customer needs.  Engages with customers to understand changing needs and requirements.  Expands approaches and strategies to market statistical and methodological services to new or potential partners or customers  							Develops and applies hypothesis-driven mathematical approaches to identify, collect, process, analyze, and visualize data to discover insights that address partner or customer needs to enhance existing products, optimize processes, and understand systems  							Develops and applies technical methods to identify, collect, process, and analyze data that spans across FBI/TSC databases and systems using tools and techniques such as data visualization, modeling, and statistical analysis to create structured data environments to address complex problem sets  							Applies statistical and mathematical algorithms to identify and model trends in structured and unstructured data.  Writes and uses scripts for modeling processes, knowledge/source capture, and conduct hypothesis testing using available tools, including Excel, STATA, R, SAS, or other programming and statistical packages  							Collaborates with an FBI Visual Information Specialists, intelligence analysts, and contract data analysts and engineers to visualize data both temporally and spatially to assist in data integrity checks, discover follow-on questions, and display analytical assessments for diverse customer sets.  Understands data well enough to know how to communicate technical data to non-technical audiences and to understand the tools to use and how to use them creatively. Conceptualizes, designs, and creates original graphics and visual displays  						     						Education and Experience Requirements:    						A degree in Mathematics, Statistics, or Actuarial Science is preferable.  The degree must be in a major field of study that is appropriate for the position.  An ideal candidate will have a PhD and at least five years of relevant experience; however, a Master’s degree with at least fifteen years of relevant experience will suffice  							Candidate must have professional skill in Applied Mathematics (e.g., probability and statistics, formal modeling, computational social sciences) and Computer Programming (e.g., math/statistics packages, programming languages, computer science, machine learning, scientific computing)  							Candidate must have professional skill in modeling real-world problems and in applying probability theory and statistical analysis to model, design, and carry out analyses   							Candidate must have professional skill in designing mathematical research methodologies, including statistical sampling and analytic methods, to conduct studies and to complete analytic projects  							Candidate must have programming experience, such as Java, C/C++, Python, SQL, R, etc. Additional background in at least one of the following three areas is required: Adobe Creative Suite, Tableau, and/or SAS  							Candidate must have skill in oral presentations and communication of instructions concerning mathematical analysis, and in writing reports or documents that convey the findings/results of mathematical analysis to non-technical audiences  						Strategic Operational Solutions, Inc. is an IT strategy and management company specializing in delivering strategic solutions that enhance business operations in the Federal sector across DoD, DHS, IC, and LEC.
